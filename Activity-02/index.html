Once upon a time, there was a little ferry named Finley.

Finley was not the fastest boat, but he had a special gift—he could always be counted on.

Every day, he helped the animals of the village cross the river to school, work, and the marketplace.

Finley loved being steady and reliable.

One day, a big storm came, and all the other boats were too scared to sail. However, Finley said I am not afraid.

He bravely carried everyone safely across the river. 

            Steadiness
            (a) Finley is reliable and always helps others cross the river.
            (b) He is consistent and predictable, earning the trust of the village animals.
            
            Bravery
            (a) Finley doesn’t hesitate to sail through the storm.
            (b) He faces danger to ensure everyone’s safety, showing courage in tough situations.

From that day on, the animals knew that with Finley around, they were always in good hands!

"It's not always about being the fastest or the biggest, but about being there when others need you the most."
